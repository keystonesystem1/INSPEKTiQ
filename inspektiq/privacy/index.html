Privacy Policy
Effective Date: April 4, 2026 · Last Updated: April 4, 2026

1. Introduction

Keystone Systems LLC ("Company," "we," "us") operates the INSPEKTiQ platform ("Platform"). This Privacy Policy describes how we collect, use, store, and protect personal information when you use our claims operations management Platform.

INSPEKTiQ processes two distinct categories of data: Adjuster account data (your personal information as a user) and claim data (policyholder and property information you enter as part of your inspection work). This policy covers both.

2. Our Role in Data Processing

Data Controller vs. Data Processor

For your account data (name, email, subscription info), Keystone Systems LLC is the data controller — we determine how it's used.

For claim data (policyholder names, property addresses, claim numbers, inspection photos/notes), Keystone Systems LLC is the data processor — you (and the firm or carrier you represent) are the data controllers. We only process this data to provide the Platform's services to you.

4. How We Use Information

4a. Adjuster Account Data

To operate the Platform: Manage your account, authenticate sessions, process subscription payments

To communicate: Send account notifications, service updates, and product announcements

To improve the Platform: Analyze usage patterns (in aggregate), diagnose technical issues, develop new features

To ensure security: Detect fraud, unauthorized access, and abuse

4b. Claim Data

To provide Platform services: Store, organize, and display your claim documentation within your account

To generate reports: Format your claim data into exportable reports

To enable photo management: Process, store, and organize photographs you capture or upload

To enable search and retrieval: Allow you to find and access past inspections within your account

What We Do NOT Do With Claim Data

We do not access, review, or analyze your claim data except as technically necessary to operate the Platform (e.g., storage, backup, indexing for search).

We do not sell claim data. Not to carriers. Not to firms. Not to anyone.

We do not use claim data to train AI models or develop products for third parties.

We do not share claim data with any third party except as described in Section 5.

5. How Information Is Shared

5a. Claim Data

Claim data is never shared with other users, other adjusters, or any external party except in the following circumstances:

With your firm (if applicable): If your account is linked to a firm account, your claim data may be visible to authorized users within that firm, as determined by the firm's account settings. This mirrors the existing workflow where adjusters submit documentation to their firm.

By your action: When you export or share a report, you are choosing to transmit that data outside the Platform. We are not responsible for data once it leaves the Platform.

Legal requirements: We may disclose claim data if required by law, subpoena, court order, or government request.

5b. Third-Party Service Providers

We use third-party service providers to operate the Platform:

Cloud hosting: Inspection data, including photographs, is stored on encrypted cloud infrastructure

Authentication: Account management and session security

Payment processing: Subscription billing (no claim data is shared with payment processors)

All service providers are contractually bound to protect your data, process it only on our behalf, and delete it upon termination of their service agreement.

6. Data Security

Given the sensitive nature of claim data, we implement enhanced security measures:

Encryption in transit: All data transmitted between your device and our servers is encrypted via TLS

Encryption at rest: Claim data and photographs are encrypted in storage

Access controls: Only you (and authorized firm users, if applicable) can access your claim data. Keystone Systems LLC employees do not have routine access to claim data.

Audit logging: Access to claim data is logged for security monitoring

Regular security assessments: We conduct periodic reviews of our security practices and infrastructure

No system is 100% secure. While we implement industry-standard protections, we cannot guarantee absolute security. You are responsible for maintaining the security of your account credentials and your device.

7. Data Retention

Account data: Retained as long as your account is active. Deleted within 30 days of account closure.

Claim data: Retained as long as your account is active. Upon account termination, you have 30 days to export your data. After the export period, claim data including photographs, notes, and reports is permanently deleted from our systems, except where retention is required by law.

Backups: Encrypted backups may retain data for up to 90 days after deletion for disaster recovery purposes, after which they are purged.

8. Your Rights

California Residents — CCPA / CPRA Rights

If you are a California resident, you have the following rights under the CCPA as amended by the CPRA:

Right to Know: You may request the categories and specific pieces of personal information we have collected about you.

Right to Delete: You may request deletion of your personal information and claim data, subject to legal exceptions and the 30-day export period.

Right to Correct: You may request correction of inaccurate personal information.

Right to Opt-Out of Sale or Sharing: We do not sell or share personal information or claim data. If this changes, we will provide an opt-out mechanism.

Right to Non-Discrimination: We will not discriminate against you for exercising your CCPA rights.

To exercise these rights, contact us at legal@keystonestack.com. We will respond within 45 days.

Important note for policyholder data: CCPA requests from policyholders regarding their data stored in your inspection files should be directed to the firm or carrier that controls the claim, not to Keystone Systems LLC. As a data processor, we will cooperate with the data controller (you or your firm) to fulfill such requests.

All Users

Access your data: View and export all claim data through your account

Correct your data: Update account information and inspection records at any time

Delete your account: Request deletion of your account and all associated data

Export your data: Download your complete inspection archive at any time

9. GPS and Location Data

If you grant camera and location permissions on your device, photographs captured through the Platform may include GPS coordinates and timestamps. This data is stored as part of the inspection record and is useful for verifying inspection locations. You can disable location services on your device at any time, though this may reduce the utility of certain Platform features.

10. Cookies

We use essential cookies necessary for authentication and session management. We do not use advertising cookies or third-party tracking pixels. If we implement analytics cookies in the future, we will update this policy and provide opt-out controls.

11. Children's Privacy

The Platform is not intended for individuals under the age of 18. We do not knowingly collect personal information from children.

12. Changes to This Policy

We may update this Privacy Policy from time to time. Material changes will be communicated via email or through the Platform. Your continued use of the Platform after changes constitutes acceptance of the updated policy.
