For Carriers

You're paying for performance you can't verify.

Your IA firms handle the claims. You pay the LAE. But when leadership asks how a firm is performing, you're working from stale reports, phone calls, and spreadsheets someone emailed last Thursday. INSPEKTiQ changes that — with a live reporting portal you can check anytime, from any device, without making a single phone call.

Preview the Portal
The Problem

Your firms report to you on their terms.

You assign claims to an IA firm and the clock starts. What happens between assignment and submission is largely invisible to you. You know when the file arrives — and you know when it doesn't. Everything in between is a black box managed by the firm, reported by the firm, on a schedule the firm controls.

When you need cycle time data for a quarterly review, someone pulls a spreadsheet. When an SLA breach happens, you find out after the fact. When you need to justify why you're outsourcing instead of handling claims in-house, the data you have is whatever your firms chose to share — and it's already old by the time you see it.

This isn't a technology failure on your end. It's an architecture problem on theirs. Their systems weren't built to share data with you in real time. They were built to manage files internally — and reporting to the carrier was an afterthought bolted on as a manual export.

The question isn't whether your firms are performing. It's whether you can prove it when leadership asks.
The Portal

Ten metrics. Always live. Always yours.

When your firm runs INSPEKTiQ, you get a carrier reporting portal — a live, bookmarkable dashboard showing exactly how that firm is performing on your claims. Not a report they assembled. Not a PDF they sent when they were ready. The actual data, calculated from every claim in the system, updated as files move.

Every metric is calculated from actual claim data — not sampled, not estimated, not self-reported. Cycle time is measured at every stage transition: intake to assigned, assigned to contacted, contacted to inspected, inspected to reviewed, reviewed to submitted. You see where time lives in the process, not just the total.

No adjuster-level data is exposed. Your firm's internal operations stay internal. What you see is performance.

How It Works

A link. A bookmark. That's it.

There's no software to install. No integration to build. No IT project to scope. Your firm gives you a secure, bookmarkable URL. You open it in any browser on any device. The data is there.

The portal updates in real time because the data comes directly from the claim files your firm is managing inside INSPEKTiQ. There's no export step, no sync delay, no manual refresh. When a claim moves from inspection to review, you see it. When a file is submitted, the cycle time is logged and the metric updates.

You can filter by time period — today, last 7 days, 30 days, 90 days, or year to date. You can drill into SLA breaches. You can compare cycle time trends week over week. The data is yours to explore whenever you need it, not whenever someone sends it to you.

Your VP asks "how is that firm performing?" You already know.
The Standard

Make visibility the requirement.

When you require your firms to run on INSPEKTiQ, you get something that doesn't exist anywhere else in this industry — standardized, real-time performance reporting across every firm in your network.

Today, every firm reports differently. Different formats, different metrics, different timelines. You can't compare Firm A's cycle time to Firm B's because the data comes from different systems measured in different ways. With INSPEKTiQ, every firm reports through the same portal, with the same metrics, calculated the same way. Apples to apples. For the first time.

SLA enforcement stops being a conversation and becomes a data point. Cycle time isn't debated — it's timestamped. Revision trends are tracked by loss type and week so you see patterns, not anecdotes. And when it's time to decide which firms get more volume, the decision is backed by data both sides can see.

This is the reporting standard the industry has never had. And once you've seen it, you'll never want to work with a firm that can't provide it.
