Phase 01 — Claims Intake

A claim arrives. The file already exists.

Imagine this. A file exists in the system before anyone has the chance to read it.

A loss notice hits your intake address. Before your team opens the email, INSPEKTiQ has already parsed it, created the file, structured the fields, and started the SLA clock. The timer has started and the carrier holds the stopwatch. No sweat.

Email-to-Intake Auto-Parsing
Forward a loss notice — the claim is auto-created, parsed into structured fields, and immediately visible across the pipeline. No human touches intake. The SLA clock starts on receipt, not on the moment someone gets around to entering it.

The file is structured. The team is notified. The SLA clock is running. And your operations staff didn't lift a finger. That's how a claim should enter your system — not through a manual process that adds half a day of cycle time before anyone even picks up the phone.

The cycle time advantage starts before anyone picks up the phone.
Phase 02 — Dispatch & Assignment

The right adjuster. Immediately.

Now you need to find your adjuster. It's right in front of you. Or better yet, circle the claim and filter for the adjuster that has the right credentials. Send the assignment. All done within minutes of the claim hitting your system — and the carrier just pushed the send button.

INSPEKTiQ feeds the system with enough information to make real-time decisions — claims color-coded by urgency, adjusters plotted by location, capacity calculated from live file data. You don't ask who's available. You see it.

Lasso Dispatch with Filtering
Draw a boundary on the map. Filter by loss type, claim category, and certification requirements. Set a max claims threshold. The platform shows you only the adjusters who qualify. Assign in seconds, not hours.
Live Adjuster Capacity
Capacity isn't self-reported — it's calculated from active file assignments in the system. You know who actually has room before you assign, not after they decline.

Every claim pin on the map is color-coded to its SLA status — green means new, orange means at risk, red means overdue. Adjuster pins show current file count. Certifications — TWIA, Flood, Commercial, Drone, Xactimate — are visible and filterable before you assign any file.

One click deploys. The adjuster gets everything on their device.
Phase 03 — Field Documentation

The inspection feeds the file.

This is where the entire architecture proves itself. Your adjuster arrives at the property. In every other workflow, what happens next is disconnected from you — photos taken on a phone, notes scribbled in a separate app, a GLR reconstructed from memory hours later at a desk, then everything uploaded manually into whatever system you're running.

INSPEKTiQ doesn't work that way. The adjuster inspects with INSPEKTiT — and the documentation is in the claim file before they leave the property.

Connected Field Inspection App
INSPEKTiT is a purpose-built field app with guided documentation, auto-labeled photo sections, and structured data collection. Not a mobile web view. Not a stripped-down afterthought. The only firm platform with its own connected inspection app.
Real-Time Photo Streaming
Photos upload into the claim file as they're taken — organized by elevation and section, labeled automatically. You watch the inspection happen live. No batch upload. No waiting.

The general loss report doesn't get written after the fact. It gets generated on-site, during the inspection, with the data the adjuster is collecting in real time.

On-Site GLR Generation
The GLR is completed during the inspection — not reconstructed at a desk hours later. The report is done before the adjuster leaves the property.
Live Inspection Progress
Status changes, photo uploads, and documentation updates are visible in the CMS in real time. You don't wait for a callback or an upload — you see the inspection happening.
The gap between the inspection and the file doesn't exist.
Phase 04 — File Management & QA

The file is the operating record.

Your documentation is in the file. Now the file needs to be managed, reviewed, and submitted. This is where most systems start showing their seams — limited statuses that don't match how claims actually move, no QA workflow, no structured tabs for the data that matters to you or your client.

The claim file in INSPEKTiQ isn't a folder with attachments. It's your complete operating record.

15 Native Claim Statuses
Including real-time updates and uploads — photos, claim-related documents, timestamps, and statuses. Real operational states that match how claims actually move through your firm. Not generic labels forced into software that wasn't built for claim mechanics.
10-Stage Visual Lifecycle
Received, Assigned, Accepted, Contacted, Scheduled, Inspected, In Review, Approved, Submitted, Closed. Every transition timestamped. Every stage visible to you, your reviewer, and your client.
Nothing is bolted on. Nothing is missing.
Phase 05 — Client Reporting

Your client sees the proof without asking.

The file is submitted. The claim is closed. But the client relationship doesn't end at submission — it depends on what happens after. Your client needs to justify your LAE to their leadership. They need cycle time data, SLA compliance, first-pass rates, and revision trends. And they need it now, not in a quarterly PDF assembled at midnight by your ops manager.

INSPEKTiQ gives your client a live window into your operation — always current, always available.

Live Carrier Reporting Portal
Cycle time, SLA compliance, first-pass rate, revision trends, pipeline distribution, and claim-level drill-down. Ten firm-level KPIs updated in real time. Every metric calculated from every claim — no sampling, no estimates.
Bookmarkable Carrier Access
Your client bookmarks a link and checks your firm's performance data anytime. No scheduled reports. No phone calls. No spreadsheets. The carrier's VP asks "how is that firm performing?" — your client already knows.
This is the feature that makes a carrier never want to use a firm that doesn't have it.
